Findings help you decide where to focus remediation work first. This page explains what findings represent, how they differ from attack paths, and how to use exposure and impact to prioritize work.

## Understand findings and attack paths

An **attack path** is a route of permissions and relationships that an attacker can use to move through your environment.

A **finding** is a specific risk instance that BloodHound identifies from those routes.

One finding can include one or more attack paths. This is why attack path counts and finding counts can differ.

## Understand relationship, list, and hygiene findings

BloodHound groups findings into types so you can reason about risk faster:

* **Relationship-based findings** are tied to connections between assets.
* **List-based findings** are tied to risky properties on an asset.
* **Hygiene findings** are broad cleanup risks that can increase overall exposure.

These types help you separate structural access risks from configuration risks.

## Understand zones and boundaries

Findings are evaluated in the context of your privilege model, including Tier Zero and additional Privilege Zones.

In practical terms, this means:

* A finding can be high priority in one zone and lower priority in another.
* Cross-zone access routes are important because they can become stepping stones to higher-privilege assets.

Use zone filters to understand where remediation work will reduce risk the most.

## Use exposure and impact together

Use both metrics when you prioritize remediation:

* **Exposure** answers: how many principals can reach a risky condition.
* **Impact** answers: how much of your environment could be affected if that condition is abused.

In general, findings with high exposure and high impact should be addressed first.

## Decide when to remediate or accept

Remediate a finding when you are ready to remove the risk.

Accept a finding when the risk is known and temporarily tolerated, such as while waiting for operational changes to complete.

Acceptance is not the same as remediation. It helps you manage workflow, while remediation reduces risk.

For step-by-step instructions, see [Accept Findings](/analyze-data/findings/accept).

## Measure progress over time

Track progress in the [Posture Page](/analyze-data/findings/posture):

* Use finding trends to monitor risk reduction.
* Use attack path trends to monitor how many routes remain.
* Use date filters consistently so comparisons are meaningful.

As findings and attack paths trend down over time, your posture improves.
